1

Lekhana purchases 84 kg of rice from one farm and 108 kg from another. She wants to pack the rice in bags with the same weight (a whole number of kg) using as few bags as possible. What should the weight of each bag be?

Solution:

Step 1: Find the factors of 84 and 108.

Factors of 84: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 12, 14, 21, 28, 42, 84

Factors of 108: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 27, 36, 54, 108

Step 2: Find common factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Step 3: To use as few bags as possible, we need the largest bag size, which is the HCF.

Step 4: HCF(84, 108) = 12

Answer: Each bag should weigh 12 kg.

Part 2: Finding LCM (Lowest Common Multiple)

7

Anshu and Guna make torans. Anshu uses strips of 6 cm and Guna uses strips of 8 cm. What is the smallest possible length of toran that both can make?

Solution:

We need to find the LCM of 6 and 8.

Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, ...

Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56, ...

Common multiples: 24, 48, ...

Smallest common multiple = 24

Or using prime factorization: 6 = 2 × 3, 8 = 2 × 2 × 2

LCM = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 24

Answer: The smallest possible length is 24 cm.

Part 3: Patterns and Properties

11

Make a general statement about the HCF for:

Solution:

(a) Two consecutive even numbers: The HCF of two consecutive even numbers is always 2.

Example: 4 and 6 have HCF = 2, 10 and 12 have HCF = 2.

(b) Two consecutive odd numbers: The HCF of two consecutive odd numbers is always 1.

Example: 3 and 5 have HCF = 1, 15 and 17 have HCF = 1.

(c) Two even numbers: The HCF of two even numbers is always at least 2.

Example: 8 and 12 have HCF = 4, 6 and 10 have HCF = 2.

(d) Two consecutive numbers: The HCF of two consecutive numbers is always 1.

Example: 7 and 8 have HCF = 1, 14 and 15 have HCF = 1.

(e) Two co-prime numbers: By definition, the HCF of two co-prime numbers is 1.

Example: 8 and 9 have HCF = 1, 15 and 16 have HCF = 1.

12

Find two numbers where the LCM is one of the two numbers. Then make a general statement about such numbers.

Solution:

Examples: (3, 24), (5, 15), (8, 16), (6, 18)

General statement: When one number is a multiple of the other, the LCM is the larger number (the multiple).

Explanation: If a is a factor of b (b is a multiple of a), then LCM(a, b) = b.

Algebraic form: If b = k × a, where k is an integer, then LCM(a, b) = b.

13

Make a general statement about the LCM for:

Solution:

(a) Two multiples of 3: The LCM of two multiples of 3 is also a multiple of 3.

Example: LCM(6, 9) = 18 (which is a multiple of 3).

(b) Two consecutive even numbers: The LCM of two consecutive even numbers is half their product.

Example: 4 and 6: Product = 24, LCM = 12 = 24 ÷ 2.

Explanation: Consecutive even numbers are of the form 2n and 2(n+1). Their LCM is 2 × n × (n+1), which is half of 2n × 2(n+1) = 4n(n+1).

(c) Two consecutive numbers: The LCM of two consecutive numbers is their product.

Example: 7 and 8: LCM = 56 = 7 × 8.

Explanation: Consecutive numbers are co-prime, so their LCM equals their product.

(d) Two co-prime numbers: The LCM of two co-prime numbers is their product.

Example: 8 and 9: LCM = 72 = 8 × 9.

Explanation: Since they have no common factors other than 1, their LCM must be their product.

Solution:

Step 2: We need a common multiple of 3, 5, and 7 that is less than 200.

Step 3: Since 3, 5, and 7 are all prime, their LCM = 3 × 5 × 7 = 105.

Find the smallest number that is divisible by 3, 4, 5 and 7, but leaves a remainder of 10 when divided by 11.

Solution:

Step 1: Find the LCM of 3, 4, 5, and 7.

3, 4, 5, and 7 are either prime or co-prime.

LCM = 3 × 4 × 5 × 7 = 420

Step 2: We need a number that is a multiple of 420 and leaves remainder 10 when divided by 11.

This means: Number = 420k, and (420k - 10) is divisible by 11.

Step 3: Test multiples of 420:

  • 420 × 1 = 420: 420 ÷ 11 = 38 remainder 2 (not 10)
  • 420 × 2 = 840: 840 ÷ 11 = 76 remainder 4 (not 10)
  • 420 × 3 = 1260: 1260 ÷ 11 = 114 remainder 6 (not 10)
  • 420 × 4 = 1680: 1680 ÷ 11 = 152 remainder 8 (not 10)
  • 420 × 5 = 2100: 2100 ÷ 11 = 190 remainder 10 ✓

Answer: The smallest number is 2100.

Verification: 2100 ÷ 3 = 700, ÷ 4 = 525, ÷ 5 = 420, ÷ 7 = 300, and 2100 ÷ 11 = 190 with remainder 10.

20

In 'Fire in the Mountain', when 6 or 9 was called, no one got out, but when 10 was called, some got out. How many children could have been playing initially?

Solution:

Step 1: When 6 or 9 was called, no one got out. This means the number of children is a multiple of both 6 and 9.

Step 2: LCM of 6 and 9 is 18. So the number of children is a multiple of 18.

Step 3: When 10 was called, some got out. This means the number of children is NOT a multiple of 10.

Step 4: Check the options which are multiples of 18 but not multiples of 10:

  • (a) 72: Multiple of 18? 72 ÷ 18 = 4 ✓. Multiple of 10? 72 ÷ 10 = 7.2 ✗. So 72 is a possible answer.
  • (b) 90: Multiple of 18? 90 ÷ 18 = 5 ✓. Multiple of 10? 90 ÷ 10 = 9 ✓. So 90 is NOT a valid answer (no one would get out when 10 is called).
  • (c) 45: Multiple of 18? 45 ÷ 18 = 2.5 ✗. So 45 is NOT valid.
  • (d) 3: Multiple of 18? No ✗.
  • (e) 36: Multiple of 18? 36 ÷ 18 = 2 ✓. Multiple of 10? 36 ÷ 10 = 3.6 ✗. So 36 is also a possible answer.

Step 5: Both 72 and 36 are valid. The question asks "How many children could have been playing?" and both are options. Looking at the options, both (a) 72 and (e) 36 are listed.

However, typically in such games, there would be more than a few children. Also, the problem says "some got out" when 10 was called, implying more than 10 children. So 36 is more likely than 72, but both are mathematically valid.

Most likely answer: Since 36 is the smaller valid option and is listed, the answer is (e) 36.

21

Which statements are true about the LCM of two different prime numbers (m, n)? (Select all that apply)

Solution:

Step 1: For two different prime numbers m and n, their LCM is m × n.

Step 2: Analyze each statement:

  • (a) Less than both numbers: FALSE. m × n is greater than both m and n (since n > 1 and m > 1).
  • (b) In between the two numbers: FALSE. m × n is greater than both, not between them.
  • (c) Greater than both numbers: TRUE. m × n is greater than both m and n.
  • (d) Less than m×n: FALSE. The LCM equals m × n, so it's not less than m × n.
  • (e) Greater than m×n: FALSE. The LCM equals m × n, so it's not greater than m × n.

Answer: Only statement (c) is true.

22

A dog is chasing a rabbit that has a head start of 150 feet. The dog jumps 9 feet each time while the rabbit jumps 7 feet. In how many leaps does the dog catch up with the rabbit?

Solution:

Step 1: Let x be the number of leaps when the dog catches the rabbit.

Step 2: Distance covered by dog in x leaps = 9x feet

Distance covered by rabbit in x leaps = 7x feet

Step 3: When the dog catches the rabbit:

Dog's distance = Rabbit's distance + Head start

9x = 7x + 150

Step 4: Solve for x:

9x - 7x = 150

2x = 150

x = 75

Answer: The dog catches up with the rabbit in 75 leaps.

Verification: After 75 leaps, dog covers 9 × 75 = 675 feet, rabbit covers 7 × 75 = 525 feet. Difference = 675 - 525 = 150 feet (the head start).

23

What is the smallest number that is a multiple of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, and 10?

Solution:

Step 1: We need to find the LCM of these numbers.

Step 2: Note that if a number is a multiple of 8, it's automatically a multiple of 2 and 4.

If it's a multiple of 9, it's automatically a multiple of 3.

If it's a multiple of 10, it's automatically a multiple of 2 and 5.

If it's a multiple of 8 and 9, it's automatically a multiple of 6 (since 6 = 2 × 3, and 8 provides 2, 9 provides 3).

Step 3: So we only need LCM of 5, 8, 9, and 10 (and 7 is not in the list).

But wait, we need to include all primes with highest powers:

Prime factorization:

  • 8 = 2³
  • 9 = 3²
  • 10 = 2 × 5
  • 5 = 5

Step 4: Highest powers: 2³, 3², 5

LCM = 2³ × 3² × 5 = 8 × 9 × 5 = 360

Step 5: Check: 360 is divisible by all numbers in the list:

360 ÷ 1 = 360, ÷ 2 = 180, ÷ 3 = 120, ÷ 4 = 90, ÷ 5 = 72, ÷ 6 = 60, ÷ 8 = 45, ÷ 9 = 40, ÷ 10 = 36.

Answer: The smallest number is 360.

24

Add the following fractions: 8/15, 1/20, 7/36, 11/63, and 1/21. Express your answer as a fraction in simplest form.

Solution:

Step 1: Find the LCM of denominators: 15, 20, 36, 63, 21

Prime factorization:

  • 15 = 3 × 5
  • 20 = 2² × 5
  • 36 = 2² × 3²
  • 63 = 3² × 7
  • 21 = 3 × 7

Step 2: LCM = highest powers: 2² × 3² × 5 × 7 = 4 × 9 × 5 × 7 = 1260

Step 3: Convert each fraction to denominator 1260:

  • 8/15 = (8 × 84)/1260 = 672/1260 (since 1260 ÷ 15 = 84)
  • 1/20 = (1 × 63)/1260 = 63/1260 (since 1260 ÷ 20 = 63)
  • 7/36 = (7 × 35)/1260 = 245/1260 (since 1260 ÷ 36 = 35)
  • 11/63 = (11 × 20)/1260 = 220/1260 (since 1260 ÷ 63 = 20)
  • 1/21 = (1 × 60)/1260 = 60/1260 (since 1260 ÷ 21 = 60)

Step 4: Add numerators: 672 + 63 + 245 + 220 + 60 = 1260

Sum = 1260/1260 = 1

Answer: The sum is 1 (or 1/1).

Interesting observation: The fractions add up to exactly 1!
