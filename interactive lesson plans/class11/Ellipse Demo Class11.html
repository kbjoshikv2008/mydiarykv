Conic Section - Ellipse

Explore the properties, equations, and components of ellipses

Definition of an Ellipse

Definition: An ellipse is the set of all points in a plane, the sum of whose distances from two fixed points (called foci) is constant.

Note: The constant (sum of distances) is always greater than the distance between the two foci.

The midpoint of the line segment joining the foci is called the center of the ellipse. The line segment through the foci is called the major axis, and the line segment through the center perpendicular to the major axis is called the minor axis. The endpoints of the major axis are called the vertices.

Relationship Between Parameters

For an ellipse, the relationship between the semi-major axis (a), semi-minor axis (b), and the distance from center to focus (c) is given by:

\( c^2 = a^2 - b^2 \quad \text{or} \quad b^2 = a^2 - c^2 \)

The eccentricity of an ellipse is defined as the ratio of the distance from center to focus (c) to the semi-major axis (a):

\( e = \frac{c}{a} \)

The eccentricity of an ellipse is always between 0 and 1. When e = 0, the ellipse is a circle.

Standard Equations of an Ellipse

Ellipse with Major Axis along X-axis

\( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \)

Where:

  • a is the length of the semi-major axis (a > b)
  • b is the length of the semi-minor axis
  • Foci are at (±c, 0) where \( c^2 = a^2 - b^2 \)
  • Vertices are at (±a, 0)

Ellipse with Major Axis along Y-axis

\( \frac{x^2}{b^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2} = 1 \)

Where:

  • a is the length of the semi-major axis (a > b)
  • b is the length of the semi-minor axis
  • Foci are at (0, ±c) where \( c^2 = a^2 - b^2 \)
  • Vertices are at (0, ±a)

Derivation of the Standard Equation

Let's derive the standard equation of an ellipse with center at the origin and foci on the x-axis.

1

Let F₁(-c, 0) and F₂(c, 0) be the foci, and P(x, y) be any point on the ellipse.

2

By definition, the sum of distances from P to the foci is constant (2a):

\( PF_1 + PF_2 = 2a \)

Using the distance formula:

\( \sqrt{(x + c)^2 + y^2} + \sqrt{(x - c)^2 + y^2} = 2a \)
3

Isolate one radical and square both sides:

\( \sqrt{(x + c)^2 + y^2} = 2a - \sqrt{(x - c)^2 + y^2} \)
\( (x + c)^2 + y^2 = 4a^2 - 4a\sqrt{(x - c)^2 + y^2} + (x - c)^2 + y^2 \)
4

Simplify and isolate the radical:

\( 4a\sqrt{(x - c)^2 + y^2} = 4a^2 - 4cx \)
\( \sqrt{(x - c)^2 + y^2} = a - \frac{c}{a}x \)
5

Square both sides again and simplify:

\( (x - c)^2 + y^2 = a^2 - 2cx + \frac{c^2}{a^2}x^2 \)
\( x^2 - 2cx + c^2 + y^2 = a^2 - 2cx + \frac{c^2}{a^2}x^2 \)
\( x^2 + y^2 + c^2 = a^2 + \frac{c^2}{a^2}x^2 \)
6

Rearrange terms:

\( x^2 - \frac{c^2}{a^2}x^2 + y^2 = a^2 - c^2 \)
\( x^2\left(1 - \frac{c^2}{a^2}\right) + y^2 = a^2 - c^2 \)
7

Since \( b^2 = a^2 - c^2 \), we get:

\( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \)

This is the standard equation of an ellipse with center at the origin and major axis along the x-axis.

Example Problems

1 Find ellipse components

Find the coordinates of the foci, vertices, lengths of axes, eccentricity, and latus rectum of the ellipse:

\( \frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{9} = 1 \)

Solution:

Comparing with the standard form \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \), we get:

\( a = 5 \), \( b = 3 \), \( c = \sqrt{a^2 - b^2} = \sqrt{25 - 9} = 4 \)

Foci: (-4, 0) and (4, 0)

Vertices: (-5, 0) and (5, 0)

Length of major axis: 10 units

Length of minor axis: 6 units

Eccentricity: \( e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{4}{5} \)

Latus rectum: \( \frac{2b^2}{a} = \frac{18}{5} \)

2 Ellipse with vertical major axis

Find the coordinates of the foci, vertices, lengths of axes, and eccentricity of the ellipse:

\( 9x^2 + 4y^2 = 36 \)

Solution:

Divide by 36 to get standard form:

\( \frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{y^2}{9} = 1 \)

Here, the larger denominator is under \( y^2 \), so the major axis is along the y-axis.

Comparing with \( \frac{x^2}{b^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2} = 1 \), we get:

\( b = 2 \), \( a = 3 \), \( c = \sqrt{a^2 - b^2} = \sqrt{9 - 4} = \sqrt{5} \)

Foci: (0, -√5) and (0, √5)

Vertices: (0, -3) and (0, 3)

Length of major axis: 6 units

Length of minor axis: 4 units

Eccentricity: \( e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3} \)

3 Find equation from vertices and foci

Find the equation of the ellipse with vertices at (±13, 0) and foci at (±5, 0).

Solution:

Since vertices are on the x-axis, the equation is of the form \( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \).

Given: \( a = 13 \), \( c = 5 \)

Then \( b^2 = a^2 - c^2 = 169 - 25 = 144 \)

So the equation is:

\( \frac{x^2}{169} + \frac{y^2}{144} = 1 \)
