Key Concepts

Before solving the problem, let's review the key concepts of 3D geometry that we'll use:

Direction Ratios

Direction ratios of a line are proportional to the direction cosines and represent the line's orientation in 3D space.

Line Equations in 3D

A line in 3D can be represented in symmetric form: \(\frac{x-x_1}{a} = \frac{y-y_1}{b} = \frac{z-z_1}{c}\)

Parallel Lines

Two lines are parallel if their direction ratios are proportional: \(\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2}\)

Cross Product

The cross product of two vectors gives a vector perpendicular to both, with magnitude equal to the area of the parallelogram they form.

Shortest Distance Between Skew Lines

For skew lines, the shortest distance is along the common perpendicular: \(d = \frac{|(\vec{a}_2 - \vec{a}_1) \cdot (\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2)|}{|\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2|}\)

Problem Statement(A Case Based question Asked in CBSE Board Exam)

An engineer is designing a new metro rail network in a city. Initially, two metro lines, Line A and Line B, each consisting of multiple stations are designed.

Line Equations

Line A: \[l_1 : \frac{x-2}{3} = \frac{y+1}{-2} = \frac{z-3}{4}\]
Line B: \[l_2 : \frac{x-1}{2} = \frac{y-3}{1} = \frac{z+2}{-3}\]

Note: The metro lines are represented as 3D lines in space. Line A passes through point (2, -1, 3) with direction ratios (3, -2, 4), while Line B passes through point (1, 3, -2) with direction ratios (2, 1, -3).

Questions & Solutions

(i) Check if the two metro tracks are parallel

Direction ratios of Line A: \(\langle 3, -2, 4 \rangle\)

Direction ratios of Line B: \(\langle 2, 1, -3 \rangle\)

Check if direction ratios are proportional:

\[ \frac{3}{2} = 1.5, \quad \frac{-2}{1} = -2, \quad \frac{4}{-3} \approx -1.33 \]

Since \(1.5 \ne -2 \ne -1.33\), the direction ratios are not proportional.

Conclusion: The two metro tracks are NOT parallel.

(ii) Equation of solar panel placement line

Solar panels are to be installed on the rooftop of the metro stations. Determine the equation of the line representing the placement of solar panels on the rooftop of Line A's stations, given that panels are to be positioned parallel to Line A's track and pass through the point \((1, -2, -3)\).

We need a line parallel to Line A passing through point \((1, -2, -3)\).

Since it's parallel to Line A, it will have the same direction ratios: \(\langle 3, -2, 4 \rangle\)

Equation of the line in Cartesian form:

\[ \frac{x-1}{3} = \frac{y+2}{-2} = \frac{z+3}{4} \]

Equation of the line in Vector form:

\[ \vec{r} = (\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} - 3\hat{k}) + \lambda (3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}) \]

(iii) Pedestrian Pathway

Choose one of the following options:

Option (a)

To connect the stations, a pedestrian pathway perpendicular to the two metro lines is to be constructed which passes through point \((3, 2, 1)\). Determine the equation of the pedestrian walkway.

The pathway is perpendicular to both Line A and Line B, so its direction vector is the cross product of their direction vectors.

Direction vector of Line A: \(\vec{b}_1 = 3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}\)

Direction vector of Line B: \(\vec{b}_2 = 2\hat{i} + \hat{j} - 3\hat{k}\)

Calculate cross product:

\[ \vec{b} = \vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2 = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 3 & -2 & 4 \\ 2 & 1 & -3 \end{vmatrix} \]

\[ = \hat{i}[(-2)(-3) - (4)(1)] - \hat{j}[(3)(-3) - (4)(2)] + \hat{k}[(3)(1) - (-2)(2)] \]

\[ = \hat{i}[6 - 4] - \hat{j}[-9 - 8] + \hat{k}[3 + 4] \]

\[ = 2\hat{i} + 17\hat{j} + 7\hat{k} \]

Equation of pathway passing through \((3, 2, 1)\):

\[ \vec{r} = (3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + \hat{k}) + \lambda (2\hat{i} + 17\hat{j} + 7\hat{k}) \]

Option (b)

Find the shortest distance between Line A and Line B.

Position vectors:

\(\vec{a}_1 = 2\hat{i} - \hat{j} + 3\hat{k}\) (point on Line A)

\(\vec{a}_2 = \hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - 2\hat{k}\) (point on Line B)

Direction vectors:

\(\vec{b}_1 = 3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}\)

\(\vec{b}_2 = 2\hat{i} + \hat{j} - 3\hat{k}\)

We already calculated:

\(\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2 = 2\hat{i} + 17\hat{j} + 7\hat{k}\)

Magnitude: \(|\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2| = \sqrt{2^2 + 17^2 + 7^2} = \sqrt{4 + 289 + 49} = \sqrt{342}\)

Vector connecting points on the two lines:

\(\vec{a}_2 - \vec{a}_1 = (1-2)\hat{i} + (3-(-1))\hat{j} + (-2-3)\hat{k} = -\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} - 5\hat{k}\)

Shortest distance formula:

\[ d = \frac{|(\vec{a}_2 - \vec{a}_1) \cdot (\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2)|}{|\vec{b}_1 \times \vec{b}_2|} \]

Calculate dot product:

\((-\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} - 5\hat{k}) \cdot (2\hat{i} + 17\hat{j} + 7\hat{k}) = (-1)(2) + (4)(17) + (-5)(7) = -2 + 68 - 35 = 31\)

Shortest distance:

\[ d = \frac{|31|}{\sqrt{342}} = \frac{31}{\sqrt{342}} \]
