Question 1: A and B are independent events such that \(P(A \cap \overline{B}) = \frac{1}{4}\) and \(P(\overline{A} \cap \overline{B}) = \frac{1}{6}\). Find P(A) and P(B). 4 marks

Question 2: Two balls are drawn at random one by one with replacement from an urn containing equal number of red balls and green balls. Find the probability distribution of number of red balls. Also, find the mean of the random variable. 4 marks

Question 3: A and B throw a die alternately till one of them gets a '6' and wins the game. Find their respective probabilities of winning, if A starts the game first. 4 marks

Question 4: There are two coins. One of them is a biased coin such that P(head) : P(tail) is 1 : 3 and the other coin is a fair coin. A coin is selected at random and tossed once. If the coin showed head, then find the probability that it is a biased coin. 4 marks

Question 5: From a lot of 30 bulbs which include 6 defective bulbs, a sample of 2 bulbs is drawn at random one by one with replacement. Find the probability distribution of the number of defective bulbs and hence find the mean number of defective bulbs. 4 marks

Question 6: In answering a question on a multiple choice test, a student either knows the answer or guesses. Let \(\frac{3}{5}\) be the probability that he knows the answer and \(\frac{2}{5}\) be the probability that he guesses. Assuming that a student who guesses at the answer will be correct with probability \(\frac{1}{3}\). What is the probability that the student knows the answer, given that he answered it correctly? 4 marks
