Matrix Fundamentals

Definition: A matrix is an ordered rectangular array of numbers or functions. Elements are arranged in rows and columns.

📐 Order of a Matrix

If a matrix has m rows and n columns, its order is \(m \times n\). Total elements = \(m \times n\).

Example: \( \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & 19 \\ 35 & -2 & 12 \end{bmatrix} \) is \(2 \times 3\).

🔢 Representation & Notation

Matrix \(A = [a_{ij}]_{m \times n}\) where \(a_{ij}\) is element in i-th row, j-th column.

Example (from PDF): Factory workers data: \( \begin{bmatrix} 30 & 25 \\ 25 & 31 \\ 27 & 26 \end{bmatrix} \) → entry (3,2) = women in factory III = 26.

Applications: Cryptography, geometry (vertices), economics, spreadsheets.

📊 Matrix Equality

Two matrices are equal iff same order and all corresponding entries equal.

Solve: \( \begin{bmatrix} x & 2y \\ z & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) ⇒ x=1, y=3, z=3.

Classification of Matrices

Column Matrix

Only 1 column: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}_{3\times1}\)

Row Matrix

Only 1 row: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}_{1\times3}\)

Square Matrix

Rows = Columns, e.g. \(3\times 3\). Diagonal: \(a_{11}, a_{22},..., a_{nn}\).

Diagonal Matrix

Non-diagonal entries zero. Example: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \)

Scalar Matrix

Diagonal matrix with equal diagonal entries: \(kI\).

Identity Matrix

\(I_n\): ones on diagonal, zeros elsewhere. \(I_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\)

Zero Matrix

All entries zero. Denoted by O.

✍️ Identify the matrix type: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \)

Properties of Addition

Matrix Multiplication (AB)

Number of columns of A = number of rows of B. Result Cik = sum of products of i-th row of A and k-th column of B.

Properties

Associative: (AB)C = A(BC)
Distributive: A(B+C)=AB+AC
Multiplicative Identity: IA = A

Zero product

AB = O does NOT imply A=O or B=O. Example: \( \begin{bmatrix}0&-1\\0&2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}3&5\\0&0\end{bmatrix} = O\)

Transpose of a Matrix & Symmetric/Skew-Symmetric

Transpose A' (or AT) : rows ↔ columns. (AB)' = B'A'.

Symmetric Matrix

A' = A → \(a_{ij}=a_{ji}\). Example: \(\begin{bmatrix}1&2\\2&3\end{bmatrix}\).

Skew-Symmetric

A' = -A → diagonal entries zero. Example: \(\begin{bmatrix}0&5\\-5&0\end{bmatrix}\).

Theorem: Any square matrix = Symmetric part \((A+A')/2\) + Skew-symmetric part \((A-A')/2\).

Invertible Matrices & Inverse

If AB = BA = I, then B = A⁻¹. Inverse is unique. (AB)⁻¹ = B⁻¹A⁻¹.
