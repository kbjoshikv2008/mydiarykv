🧠 Introduction

Integration is the inverse process of differentiation. If we are given the derivative of a function, integration helps us find the original function (called the anti‑derivative or primitive).

$$ \int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + C \quad \text{where} \quad F'(x) = f(x) $$

C is the constant of integration — any real number.

  • Indefinite integral → family of functions (differ by a constant)
  • Integrand → $f(x)$  ·  Variable → $x$
  • Integration is used in physics, economics, engineering, and more.

🔑 Properties of Indefinite Integrals

PropertyExpression
Differentiation & integration are inverses $$ \frac{d}{dx}\left[\int f(x)\,dx\right] = f(x) $$
Sum / Difference $$ \int [f(x) \pm g(x)]\,dx = \int f(x)\,dx \pm \int g(x)\,dx $$
Constant multiple $$ \int k \cdot f(x)\,dx = k \cdot \int f(x)\,dx $$
Linear combination $$ \int \left[k_1 f_1 + \cdots + k_n f_n\right] dx = k_1 \int f_1 + \cdots + k_n \int f_n $$

🔧 Methods of Integration

We use different techniques depending on the form of the integrand.

1. Inspection (Recognition)

Identify a function whose derivative is the integrand.

Example $$ \int 2x \cos(x^2)\,dx \quad \rightarrow \quad \text{put } t = x^2,\; dt = 2x\,dx $$ $$ \rightarrow \int \cos t\,dt = \sin t + C = \sin(x^2) + C $$

2. Substitution

Change variable to simplify the integral.

Example $$ \int x\sqrt{x+2}\,dx \quad \rightarrow \quad \text{put } x+2 = t^2,\; dx = 2t\,dt,\; x = t^2-2 $$ $$ = \int (t^2-2)\cdot t \cdot 2t\,dt = 2\int (t^4 - 2t^2)\,dt $$ $$ = 2\left(\frac{t^5}{5} - \frac{2t^3}{3}\right) + C = \frac{2}{5}(x+2)^{5/2} - \frac{4}{3}(x+2)^{3/2} + C $$

3. Integration by Parts

$$ \int u \cdot v\,dx = u \cdot \int v\,dx - \int \left[u' \cdot \int v\,dx\right] dx $$

Example $$ \int x e^x\,dx \quad \rightarrow \quad u = x,\; v = e^x $$ $$ = x \cdot e^x - \int 1 \cdot e^x\,dx = e^x(x-1) + C $$

4. Partial Fractions

Decompose rational functions into simpler fractions.

Example $$ \int \frac{dx}{(x+1)(x+2)} = \int \left[\frac{1}{x+1} - \frac{1}{x+2}\right] dx $$ $$ = \ln|x+1| - \ln|x+2| + C = \ln\left|\frac{x+1}{x+2}\right| + C $$

5. Trigonometric Identities

Use identities like $\sin^2 x = \frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}$, etc.

Example $$ \int \sin^2 x\,dx = \int \frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}\,dx = \frac{x}{2} - \frac{\sin 2x}{4} + C $$

⭐ Important Questions

Click a question to reveal the solution step-by-step
Q1 $$ \int (3x^2 + 2x + 5)\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 Use the sum rule: $$ \int 3x^2\,dx + \int 2x\,dx + \int 5\,dx $$
Step 2 Apply power rule: $$ 3\cdot \frac{x^3}{3} + 2\cdot \frac{x^2}{2} + 5x $$
$$ = x^3 + x^2 + 5x + C $$
Q2 $$ \int \sin x \cos x\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 Use the identity: $\sin x \cos x = \frac{1}{2}\sin 2x$
Step 2 $$ \int \frac{1}{2}\sin 2x\,dx = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \left(-\frac{\cos 2x}{2}\right) $$
$$ = -\frac{1}{4}\cos 2x + C $$
💡 Alternate: put $t = \sin x \rightarrow dt = \cos x\,dx \rightarrow \int t\,dt = \frac{t^2}{2} = \frac{\sin^2 x}{2} + C$
(Both answers differ by a constant.)
Q3 $$ \int x e^x\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 (by parts) $u = x \rightarrow u' = 1$  ·  $v = e^x \rightarrow \int v\,dx = e^x$
Step 2 $$ \int x e^x\,dx = x \cdot e^x - \int 1 \cdot e^x\,dx $$
$$ = e^x(x - 1) + C $$
Q4 $$ \int \frac{dx}{(x+1)(x+2)} $$ ▼
Step 1 (partial fractions) $$ \frac{1}{(x+1)(x+2)} = \frac{A}{x+1} + \frac{B}{x+2} $$ $$ \rightarrow 1 = A(x+2) + B(x+1) \rightarrow A=1,\; B=-1 $$
Step 2 $$ \int \left[\frac{1}{x+1} - \frac{1}{x+2}\right] dx $$
$$ = \ln|x+1| - \ln|x+2| + C = \ln\left|\frac{x+1}{x+2}\right| + C $$
Q5 $$ \int \sin^2 x\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 (trig identity) $$ \sin^2 x = \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{2} $$
Step 2 $$ \int \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{2}\,dx = \frac{1}{2}\int dx - \frac{1}{2}\int \cos 2x\,dx $$
$$ = \frac{x}{2} - \frac{\sin 2x}{4} + C $$
Q6 $$ \int x\sqrt{x+2}\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 (substitution) Put $t = \sqrt{x+2} \rightarrow x = t^2-2,\; dx = 2t\,dt$
Step 2 $$ \int (t^2-2)\cdot t \cdot 2t\,dt = 2\int (t^4 - 2t^2)\,dt $$
Step 3 $$ 2\left(\frac{t^5}{5} - \frac{2t^3}{3}\right) + C $$
$$ = \frac{2}{5}(x+2)^{5/2} - \frac{4}{3}(x+2)^{3/2} + C $$
Q7 $$ \int e^x (\sin x + \cos x)\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 (recall formula) $$ \int e^x \left[f(x) + f'(x)\right] dx = e^x f(x) + C $$
Step 2 Here $f(x) = \sin x$, $f'(x) = \cos x$
$$ = e^x \sin x + C $$
Q8 $$ \int \tan x\,dx $$ ▼
Step 1 $$ \tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \quad \rightarrow \quad \text{put } t = \cos x,\; dt = -\sin x\,dx $$
Step 2 $$ \int \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\,dx = -\int \frac{dt}{t} = -\ln|t| + C $$
$$ = -\ln|\cos x| + C = \ln|\sec x| + C $$

📊 Summary & Key Takeaways

🔁 Inverse Process

Integration reverses differentiation.
$$ \int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + C \;\Leftrightarrow\; F'(x) = f(x) $$

📐 Standard Formulas

Power, exponential, trigonometric, and inverse trig — memorise them for quick recall.

🧩 Key Techniques

Substitution · Parts · Partial Fractions · Trigonometric Identities.
Choose wisely based on the integrand.

🧠 Constant of Integration

Always add C (any real number). It represents the family of all anti‑derivatives.

📖 NCERT Connection

Chapter 7 covers indefinite integrals in detail. Focus on exercises 7.1–7.6 for mastery.

🎯 Real‑World Use

Physics (kinematics), Economics (cost functions), Engineering (area, volume), and more.
