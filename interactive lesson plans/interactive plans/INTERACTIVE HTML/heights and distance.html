Key Concepts

Before solving the problem, let's review the key concepts of trigonometry:

Angle of Elevation

The angle between the horizontal line of sight and the line of sight to an object above the horizontal.

Trigonometric Ratios

In a right triangle: sinθ = opposite/hypotenuse
cosθ = adjacent/hypotenuse
tanθ = opposite/adjacent

Height and Distance Problems

Using trigonometry to calculate unknown heights or distances by forming right triangles.

Important Angles

Special trigonometric values: tan 45° = 1, tan 30° = 1/√3, tan 60° = √3

Problem Statement (CBSE Board Exam Question)

India Gate (formerly known as All India War Memorial) is located near Kartavya Path (formerly Rajpath) at New Delhi. It stands as a memorial to 74,187 soldiers of Indian Army who gave their lives in the First World War. This 42m tall structure was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens in the style of Roman triumphal arches. A student Shreya of height 1m visited India Gate as part of her study tour.

Given Information

Height of India Gate: 42 m
Shreya's height: 1 m
Shreya's eye level: 1 m above ground

Note: When calculating angles of elevation, we need to consider the height difference between Shreya's eye level and the top of India Gate, which is 42 - 1 = 41m.

Questions & Solutions

(i) Angle of elevation at 41m distance

Instructions: can visualise angle of elevation through GeoGebra Click here.

What is the angle of elevation from Shreya's eye to the top of India Gate, if she is standing at a distance of 41m away from India Gate?

Height difference between Shreya's eye level and top of India Gate:

42 m - 1 m = 41 m

Distance from India Gate: 41 m

Using the tangent ratio:

tanθ = opposite/adjacent = 41/41 = 1

We know that tan 45° = 1, so:

θ = 45°

Answer: The angle of elevation is 45°.

(ii) Distance when angle is 60°

If Shreya observes the angle of elevation from her eye to the top of India Gate to be 60°, then how far is she standing from the base of India Gate?

Height difference between Shreya's eye level and top of India Gate:

42 m - 1 m = 41 m

Angle of elevation: 60°

Using the tangent ratio:

tan 60° = opposite/adjacent = 41/d

where d is the distance from India Gate.

We know that tan 60° = √3, so:

√3 = 41/d

Solving for d:

d = 41/√3 m

Rationalizing the denominator:

d = 41√3/3 m

Answer: Shreya is standing 41√3/3 m away from India Gate.

(iii) Choose one option

Choose one of the following options:

Option (a)

If the angle of elevation from Shreya's eye changes from 45° to 30°, when she moves some distance back from the original position. Find the distance she moves back.

Height difference between Shreya's eye level and top of India Gate:

42 m - 1 m = 41 m

Initial position: angle of elevation = 45°

Using tangent ratio:

tan 45° = 41/d₁ ⇒ 1 = 41/d₁ ⇒ d₁ = 41 m

Final position: angle of elevation = 30°

Using tangent ratio:

tan 30° = 41/d₂ ⇒ 1/√3 = 41/d₂ ⇒ d₂ = 41√3 m

Distance moved back:

d₂ - d₁ = 41√3 - 41 = 41(√3 - 1) m

Answer: Shreya moved back 41(√3 - 1) m.

Option (b)

If Shreya moves to a point which is at a distance of 41/√3 from the India Gate, then find the angle of elevation made by her eye to the top of India Gate.

Height difference between Shreya's eye level and top of India Gate:

42 m - 1 m = 41 m

Distance from India Gate: 41/√3 m

Using the tangent ratio:

tanθ = opposite/adjacent = 41/(41/√3) = √3

We know that tan 60° = √3, so:

θ = 60°

Answer: The angle of elevation is 60°.
