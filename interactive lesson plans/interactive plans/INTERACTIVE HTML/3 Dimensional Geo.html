Question 1: Check whether the lines given by equations \( x=2\lambda + 2, y=7\lambda + 1, z=-3\lambda - 3 \) and \( x=-\mu - 2, y=2\mu + 8, z=4\mu + 5 \) are perpendicular to each other or not. 4 marks

Question 2: Find the vector and the Cartesian equations of a line passing through the point (1, 2, -4) and parallel to the line joining the points A(3, 3, -5) and B(1, 0, -11). Hence, find the distance between the two lines. 4 marks

Question 3: Find the equations of the diagonals of the parallelogram PQRS whose vertices are \(P(4, 2, -6)\), \(Q(5, -3, 1)\), \(R(12, 4, 5)\) and \(S(11, 9, -2)\). Use these equations to find the point of intersection of diagonals. 4 marks

Question 4: Find the value of \(b\) so that the lines \(\frac{x - 1}{2} = \frac{y - b}{3} = \frac{z - 3}{4}\) and \(\frac{x - 4}{5} = \frac{y - 1}{2} = z\) are intersecting lines. Also, find the point of intersection of these given lines. 4 marks

Question 5: Find the coordinates of the foot of the perpendicular drawn from the point \( P(0, 2, 3) \) to the line \(\frac{x+3}{5} = \frac{y-1}{2} = \frac{z+4}{3}\). 4 marks

Question 6: Find the shortest distance between the lines \(\frac{x-8}{3} = \frac{y+9}{-16} = \frac{z-10}{7}\) and \(\frac{x-15}{3} = \frac{y-29}{8} = \frac{z-5}{-5}\). 4 marks
