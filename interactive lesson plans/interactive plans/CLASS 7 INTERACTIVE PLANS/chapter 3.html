Tile Problem: Finding HCF

Sameeksha is building her new house. The main room is 12 ft by 16 ft. She wants to cover the floor with square tiles of the same size, using as few tiles as possible. What size tiles should she buy?

Problem: Find the largest square tile that can exactly cover a 12 ft × 16 ft room. The tile size must be a whole number of feet.

Hint: The tile side must be a factor of both the length and width. List the factors of each number and find the common factors. The largest common factor is the HCF.

Prime Factorization

Any number can be written as a product of primes. This is called prime factorization. Use the division method to find the prime factors of a number.

Example: 90 = 2 × 3 × 3 × 5 = 2 × 3² × 5

Hint: Start by dividing the number by the smallest prime (2) until it's no longer divisible, then move to the next prime (3, 5, 7, etc.).

HCF using Prime Factorization

Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of two numbers using their prime factorizations. The HCF is the product of the common primes raised to the lowest power.

Example: For 45 = 3² × 5 and 75 = 3 × 5², the common primes are 3 and 5. The lowest powers are 3¹ and 5¹, so HCF = 3 × 5 = 15.

Hint: Find the prime factorization of both numbers. Identify the common prime factors and take the smallest exponent for each common prime.

4

LCM using Prime Factorization

Find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) of two numbers using prime factorization. The LCM is the product of all primes raised to the highest power present in any of the numbers.

Example: For 12 = 2² × 3 and 18 = 2 × 3², the LCM = 2² × 3² = 4 × 9 = 36.

Hint: Find the prime factorization of both numbers. For each prime factor, take the highest power that appears in either number.

Toran Problem: Finding LCM

Anshu and Guna make torans out of strips of cloth. Anshu uses strips of length 6 cm and Guna uses strips of 8 cm. If both have to make torans of the same length, what is the smallest possible length the torans could be?

Problem: Find the smallest common multiple of the strip lengths to determine the shortest toran length they can both make.

Hint: List the multiples of each strip length until you find a common multiple. The smallest common multiple is the LCM.
