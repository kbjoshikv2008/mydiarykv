MATHEMATICS – Grade 7 SET B
Expressions, Brackets & Properties
⏱️ Time: 25 min 📋 Total: 10 Marks \(4 \times 1 + 3 \times 2\)
💡 Read carefully. Use terms, brackets and properties. Write answers in space provided. No calculators.

🔹 Section A (1 mark each)

1. Identify the terms in the expression: \(5 \times 6 + 3 - 2\) (convert subtraction to addition of inverse first). 1
2. Evaluate using the correct order (multiplication and division before addition/subtraction): \(48 - 10 \times 2 + 16 \div 2\). 1
3. Fill in the blank: \(8 \times \_\_\_ = 64 \div 2\). 1
4. Write the expression for: "The total cost when you buy 4 notebooks at ₹35 each and 2 pens at ₹10 each". 1

🔸 Section B (2 marks each)

5. Compare \(113 - 25\) and \(112 - 24\) using reasoning (without calculating). Are they equal? Which is greater? Explain with a marble story or logical reasoning. 2
6. Remove the brackets and write an equivalent expression, then evaluate: \(500 - (250 - 100)\). Show the steps as \(500 - 250 + 100\). 2
7. Use the distributive property to evaluate \(104 \times 15\) quickly. Write as \((100 + 4) \times 15\) and simplify. 2
Set B – 10 marks | Show working for 2-mark questions.

🔐 ANSWER KEY – Set A & Set B

✅ SET A SOLUTIONS

  • 1. Convert: \(28 + (-7) + 8\). Terms: \(28\), \(-7\), \(8\).
  • 2. \(30 + 5 \times 4 = 30 + 20 = 50\).
  • 3. \(13 + 4 = 17\), so \(17 = \_\_ + 6\) → blank = \(11\).
  • 4. \(3 \times 12 + 2 \times 5\) or \(36 + 10\).
  • 5. \(1023 + 125 < 1022 + 128\). Reasoning: Raja had 1 more marble initially but Joy got 3 more → Joy ends with 2 more.
  • 6. \(200 - (40 + 3) = 200 - 40 - 3 = 160 - 3 = 157\).
  • 7. \(97 \times 25 = (100 - 3) \times 25 = 100 \times 25 - 3 \times 25 = 2500 - 75 = 2425\).

✅ SET B SOLUTIONS

  • 1. \(5 \times 6 + 3 - 2 = (5 \times 6) + 3 + (-2)\). Terms: \(5 \times 6\), \(3\), \(-2\).
  • 2. \(48 - 10 \times 2 + 16 \div 2 = 48 - 20 + 8 = 28 + 8 = 36\). (Multiplication/division first)
  • 3. \(64 \div 2 = 32\), so \(8 \times 4 = 32\) → blank = \(4\).
  • 4. \(4 \times 35 + 2 \times 10\) or \(140 + 20\).
  • 5. \(113 - 25 = 112 - 24\) (both equal 88). Reasoning: Raja had 1 more marble but also lost 1 more → equal.
  • 6. \(500 - (250 - 100) = 500 - 250 + 100 = 250 + 100 = 350\).
  • 7. \(104 \times 15 = (100 + 4) \times 15 = 100 \times 15 + 4 \times 15 = 1500 + 60 = 1560\).
