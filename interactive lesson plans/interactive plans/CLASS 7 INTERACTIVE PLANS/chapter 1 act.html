Chapter Summary

Key Concepts

  • Congruent figures have the same shape and size and can be superimposed exactly.
  • While checking for congruence, a figure can be rotated or flipped before superimposing.
  • For triangles, several conditions guarantee congruence:
    • SSS (Side-Side-Side): All three sides equal
    • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): Two sides and the included angle equal
    • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): Two angles and the included side equal
    • AAS (Angle-Angle-Side): Two angles and a non-included side equal
    • RHS (Right-Hypotenuse-Side): Right angle, hypotenuse, and one side equal
  • SSA (Side-Side-Angle) does NOT always guarantee congruence.
  • When triangles are congruent, their corresponding vertices, sides, and angles are equal.
  • In an isosceles triangle, angles opposite equal sides are equal.
  • In an equilateral triangle, all angles are equal to 60°.

Real-World Applications

Congruent triangles are used in:

  • Architecture and construction - Ensuring structural elements match
  • Engineering - Creating identical parts
  • Art and design - Creating symmetrical patterns
  • Navigation and surveying - Calculating distances
