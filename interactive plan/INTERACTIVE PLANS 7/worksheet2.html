Key Definitions

Intersecting Lines: Lines that cross each other at a point. The point where they cross is called the point of intersection.

Parallel Lines: Lines in the same plane that do not meet, no matter how far they are extended.

Transversal: A line that intersects two or more lines at distinct points.

Perpendicular Lines: Lines that intersect at right angles (90°).

Linear Pair: Adjacent angles that form a straight line (sum to 180°).

Vertically Opposite Angles: Angles opposite each other when two lines intersect. They are always equal.

Section A: Basic Concepts

1. How many angles are formed when two lines intersect?

Answer: Four angles are formed when two lines intersect.

2. Can two straight lines intersect at more than one point?

Answer: No, two straight lines can intersect at only one point.

3. In Fig. 5.2, if ∠a is 120°, what are the measurements of ∠b, ∠c and ∠d?

Answer: ∠b = 60°, ∠c = 120°, ∠d = 60°

Explanation: ∠a and ∠b form a linear pair (sum to 180°), so ∠b = 60°. ∠b and ∠c form a linear pair, so ∠c = 120°. ∠c and ∠d form a linear pair, so ∠d = 60°.

4. Is it always true that vertically opposite angles are equal for any pair of intersecting lines?

Answer: Yes, vertically opposite angles are always equal for any pair of intersecting lines.

5. Can you draw a pair of intersecting lines such that all four angles are equal? What is the measure of each angle?

Answer: Yes, when two lines are perpendicular, all four angles are equal and each measures 90°.

Section B: Parallel Lines

6. Name some parallel lines you can spot in your classroom.

Possible Answers: Opposite edges of a book, window frames, floor tiles, etc.

7. How would you describe the opposite edges of a square sheet of paper?

Answer: The opposite edges of a square sheet are parallel to each other.

8. How would you describe the adjacent edges of a square sheet of paper?

Answer: The adjacent edges of a square sheet are perpendicular to each other.

Section C: Transversals and Angles

9. Is it possible for all eight angles formed by a transversal intersecting two lines to have different measurements?

Answer: No, it's not possible. When a transversal intersects two lines, it forms eight angles with a maximum of four distinct angle measures.

10. What are corresponding angles?

Answer: Corresponding angles are angles that are in the same relative position at each intersection where a transversal crosses two lines.

11. When are two lines parallel with respect to the angles formed by a transversal?

Answer: Two lines are parallel when the corresponding angles formed by a transversal are equal, or when the alternate interior angles are equal.

12. What is the relationship between interior angles on the same side of a transversal intersecting parallel lines?

Answer: Interior angles on the same side of a transversal intersecting parallel lines are supplementary (sum to 180°).

Section D: Problem Solving

13. In Fig. 5.26, parallel lines l and m are intersected by transversal t. If ∠6 is 135°, what are the measures of the other angles?

Answer: ∠1 = 45°, ∠2 = 135°, ∠3 = 45°, ∠4 = 135°, ∠5 = 45°, ∠6 = 135°, ∠7 = 45°, ∠8 = 135°

Explanation: ∠6 and ∠2 are corresponding angles, so ∠2 = 135°. ∠6 and ∠8 are vertically opposite, so ∠8 = 135°. ∠6 and ∠5 form a linear pair, so ∠5 = 45°. Similarly, all other angles can be found.

14. In Fig. 5.27, lines l and m are intersected by transversal t. If ∠a is 120° and ∠f is 70°, are lines l and m parallel to each other?

Answer: No, lines l and m are not parallel.

Explanation: ∠a = 120°, so ∠b = 60° (linear pair). ∠b is a corresponding angle of ∠f. If l and m were parallel, ∠b should equal ∠f, but 60° ≠ 70°.

15. In Fig. 5.28, parallel lines l and m are intersected by transversal t. If ∠3 is 50°, what is the measure of ∠6?

Answer: ∠6 = 130°

Explanation: ∠3 and ∠6 are interior angles on the same side of the transversal, so they sum to 180°. Therefore, ∠6 = 180° - 50° = 130°.

16. In Fig. 5.29, line segment AB is parallel to CD and AD is parallel to BC. ∠DAC is 65° and ∠ADC is 60°. What are the measures of angles ∠CAB, ∠ABC, and ∠BCD?

Answer: ∠CAB = 55°, ∠ABC = 60°, ∠BCD = 120°

Explanation: ∠ADC + ∠DAB = 180° (interior angles on same side of transversal AD), so ∠DAB = 120°. ∠DAB = ∠DAC + ∠CAB, so 120° = 65° + ∠CAB, thus ∠CAB = 55°. ∠ADC + ∠BCD = 180° (interior angles on same side of transversal CD), so ∠BCD = 120°. ∠ABC = ∠ADC = 60° (alternate interior angles).
