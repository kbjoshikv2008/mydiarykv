📅 Week 1 (10 Dec – 16 Dec 2025)

Topic: Multiplication of Fractions – Understanding the Concept

Activity 1: Multiplication as Repeated Addition

Objective: To understand that multiplying a fraction by a whole number is the same as repeated addition.

Example Problem:
Aaron's tortoise walks \(\frac{1}{4}\) km in 1 hour. How far does it walk in 3 hours?

Step-by-step Solution:

1. Distance in 1 hour = \(\frac{1}{4}\) km
2. Distance in 3 hours = \(\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4}\)
3. Add numerators: \(1 + 1 + 1 = 3\)
4. Keep denominator same: \(\frac{3}{4}\)
5. Answer: \(\frac{3}{4}\) km

Practice Problems:

1. Tenzin drinks \(\frac{1}{2}\) glass of milk every day. How many glasses does he drink in:

  • 1 week (7 days)?
  • The month of January (31 days)?

2. A team of workers can make \(\frac{1}{8}\) km of water canal in 1 day. How much can they make in:

  • 5 days?
  • 2 weeks (assuming 5 working days per week)?

Activity 2: Visualizing Fraction Multiplication

Objective: To use diagrams to understand fraction multiplication.

Materials Needed: Graph paper, colored pencils

Instructions:

  1. Draw a rectangle representing 1 whole (make it 8 cm × 8 cm)
  2. To show \(\frac{1}{4}\):
    3. - Divide the rectangle into 4 equal vertical strips
    - Shade 1 strip
  3. To show \(3 \times \frac{1}{4}\):
    4. - Make 3 copies of your shaded strip
    - Place them side by side
    - Count what fraction of the whole is shaded

Practice Problems (draw diagrams for each):

  1. Show \(2 \times \frac{1}{3}\)
  2. Show \(4 \times \frac{2}{5}\)
  3. Show \(\frac{3}{7} \times 2\)

Activity 2: Real-life Applications

Objective: To apply fraction multiplication to real situations.

Problem 1: Internet Charges
1 hour of internet costs ₹8. How much will \(1\frac{1}{4}\) hours cost?

Steps:

1. Convert mixed fraction: \(1\frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{4}\)
2. Cost = \(\frac{5}{4} \times 8\)
3. Calculate: \(\frac{5 \times 8}{4} = \frac{40}{4} = 10\)
4. Answer: ₹10

Practice Problems:

📅 Week 3 (24 Dec – 30 Dec 2025)

Topic: Multiplying Two Fractions

Activity 1: Visual Multiplication Using Grids

Objective: To understand fraction × fraction using area models.

Materials Needed: Grid paper (1 cm squares), colored pencils

Example: Find \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4}\)

Instructions:

1. Draw a 4×4 grid (16 squares) representing 1 whole
2. Shade \(\frac{1}{4}\) of the grid (4 squares vertically)
3. Now, shade \(\frac{1}{2}\) of those shaded squares with a different color
4. Count the double-shaded squares: 2 squares
5. Out of 16 total squares, 2 are double-shaded = \(\frac{2}{16} = \frac{1}{8}\)
6. So, \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{8}\)

Practice Problems (use grid method):

  1. \(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{5}\)
  2. \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{1}{4}\)
  3. \(\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{5}\)

Activity 2: Discovering the Multiplication Rule for Two Fractions

Objective: To find the general rule for multiplying two fractions.

Complete this investigation table:

Problem Grid Size First Fraction Shaded Second Fraction of Shaded Double-Shaded Result Pattern
\(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4}\) 2×4=8 4 squares 2 squares 2 squares \(\frac{2}{8}=\frac{1}{4}\) \(\frac{1×1}{2×4}=\frac{1}{8}\)
\(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{5}\) 3×5=15 5 squares 3 squares 3 squares \(\frac{3}{15}=\frac{1}{5}\)
\(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4}\) 3×4=12 8 squares 9 squares 6 squares \(\frac{6}{12}=\frac{1}{2}\)

Rule: \(\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{□ \times □}{□ \times □}\)

Practice Problems (use the rule):

📅 Week 4 (31 Dec – 6 Jan 2026)

Topic: Simplifying Before Multiplying

Activity 1: The Cancellation Method

Objective: To learn how to simplify fractions before multiplying.

Example: Multiply \(\frac{12}{7} \times \frac{5}{24}\)

Method 1 (Multiply then simplify):

1. Multiply numerators: \(12 \times 5 = 60\)
2. Multiply denominators: \(7 \times 24 = 168\)
3. Result: \(\frac{60}{168}\)
4. Simplify: \(\frac{60÷12}{168÷12} = \frac{5}{14}\)

Method 2 (Simplify first):

1. Look for common factors: 12 and 24 share factor 12
2. Cancel: \(\frac{12}{7} \times \frac{5}{24} = \frac{1}{7} \times \frac{5}{2}\)
3. Multiply: \(\frac{1 \times 5}{7 \times 2} = \frac{5}{14}\)

Practice Problems (simplify first, then multiply):

  1. \(\frac{14}{15} \times \frac{25}{42}\)
  2. \(\frac{18}{35} \times \frac{21}{27}\)
  3. \(\frac{16}{25} \times \frac{15}{32}\)
  4. \(\frac{9}{20} \times \frac{25}{36}\)

Activity 2: Real-life Problems with Simplification

Problems:

1. A water tank gets \(\frac{7}{10}\) filled in 1 hour. How much gets filled in:

  • \(\frac{1}{3}\) hour?
  • \(\frac{2}{3}\) hour?
  • \(\frac{3}{4}\) hour?

2. Tsewang plants 4 saplings in a row with \(\frac{3}{4}\) m between them. What is the distance between first and last sapling?

Hint: With 4 saplings, there are 3 gaps between them.

📅 Week 5 (7 Jan – 13 Jan 2026)

Topic: Division of Fractions

Activity 1: Understanding Division as Multiplication by Reciprocal

Objective: To understand why dividing by a fraction is same as multiplying by its reciprocal.

Discovery Activity:
Complete this table:

Division As Multiplication Reciprocal Used Result
\(6 ÷ 3\) \(6 \times \frac{1}{3}\) \(\frac{1}{3}\) 2
\(1 ÷ \frac{2}{3}\) \(1 \times \frac{3}{2}\) \(\frac{3}{2}\) \(\frac{3}{2}\)
\(3 ÷ \frac{2}{3}\) \(3 \times \frac{3}{2}\)
\(\frac{1}{5} ÷ \frac{1}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{5} \times \frac{2}{1}\)
\(\frac{2}{3} ÷ \frac{3}{5}\)

Rule: To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
Reciprocal of \(\frac{a}{b}\) is \(\frac{b}{a}\)

Activity 2: Word Problems on Division

Problems:

1. Maria has 8 meters of lace. She uses \(\frac{1}{4}\) meter for each bag. How many bags can she decorate?

Think: This is \(8 ÷ \frac{1}{4} = 8 \times 4 = 32\) bags

2. \(\frac{1}{2}\) meter of ribbon makes 8 badges. How much ribbon is used for each badge?

Think: This is \(\frac{1}{2} ÷ 8 = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{8} = \frac{1}{16}\) meter

3. A baker needs \(\frac{1}{6}\) kg flour for 1 loaf. He has 5 kg flour. How many loaves can he make?

Think: This is \(5 ÷ \frac{1}{6} = 5 \times 6 = 30\) loaves

Practice Problems:

  1. Leena used \(\frac{1}{4}\) liter milk for 5 cups of tea. How much milk per cup?
  2. If \(\frac{1}{4}\) kg flour makes 12 rotis, how much flour for 6 rotis?
  3. A car travels 16 km on 1 liter petrol. How far on \(2\frac{3}{4}\) liters?

📅 Week 6 (14 Jan – 20 Jan 2026)

Topic: Review and Mixed Applications

Activity 1: Multi-step Fraction Problems

Problems:

1. Mira reads \(\frac{1}{5}\) of a 400-page book yesterday and \(\frac{3}{10}\) today. How many pages left?

Steps:

- Yesterday: \(\frac{1}{5} \times 400 = 80\) pages
- Today: \(\frac{3}{10} \times 400 = 120\) pages
- Total read: 80 + 120 = 200 pages
- Left: 400 - 200 = 200 pages

2. Train takes \(5\frac{1}{6}\) hours, plane takes \(\frac{1}{2}\) hour. How much time saved by plane?

Steps:

- Convert mixed: \(5\frac{1}{6} = \frac{31}{6}\)
- Difference: \(\frac{31}{6} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{31}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{28}{6} = 4\frac{2}{3}\) hours

Activity 2: The Dramma-tic Donation (Historical Problem)

Problem from Bhaskaracharya's Lilavati (1150 CE):
A miser gave \(\frac{1}{5}\) of \(\frac{1}{16}\) of \(\frac{1}{4}\) of \(\frac{1}{2}\) of \(\frac{2}{3}\) of \(\frac{3}{4}\) of a dramma.
1 dramma = 1280 cowrie shells. How many cowries given?

Solution:

1. Multiply all fractions: \(\frac{1}{5} \times \frac{1}{16} \times \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4}\)
2. Cancel common factors:
   - 2 in numerator and denominator
   - 3 in numerator and denominator
3. Result: \(\frac{1}{5} \times \frac{1}{16} \times \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{5 \times 16 \times 4 \times 4} = \frac{1}{1280}\)
4. Fraction of dramma: \(\frac{1}{1280}\)
5. Cowries: \(\frac{1}{1280} \times 1280 = 1\) cowrie shell

Coin Conversion Problem:

If 1 gold dinar = 12 silver drammas, 1 dramma = 4 copper panas, 1 pana = 30 cowrie shells, find:

  1. 1 cowrie = ______ copper panas
  2. 1 cowrie = ______ gold dinar

Activity 3: Pattern Discovery

Investigate this pattern:

  1. Calculate \(1 - \frac{1}{2}\)
  2. Calculate \((1 - \frac{1}{2}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{3})\)
  3. Calculate \((1 - \frac{1}{2}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{3}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{4})\)
  4. Continue up to \((1 - \frac{1}{10})\)

What pattern do you notice? Write a general formula for:
\((1 - \frac{1}{2}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{3}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{4}) \times ... \times (1 - \frac{1}{n})\)
