Forest Cover in Uttarakhand (2007-2021)

Year Forest Cover (sq km)
2007 23,243
2009 23,260
2011 23,938
2013 24,508
2015 24,240
2017 24,465
2019 24,493
2021 24,305

Observations:

  • Between 2007 and 2013, there was a gradual increase in forest cover, peaking at 24,508 sq km in 2013.
  • A decline of 268 sq km was observed between 2013 and 2015, attributed to developmental activities and rotational felling.
  • Subsequent years saw minor fluctuations, with a slight decrease noted in 2021.

Protected Areas in Uttarakhand

What are National Parks? Click to find out!

National Parks:

  • Jim Corbett National Park (Nainital & Pauri Garhwal)
    • India's first national park (est. 1936)
    • Famous for: Bengal tiger, elephants, leopards
    • Part of the Corbett Tiger Reserve
  • Nanda Devi National Park (Chamoli)
    • UNESCO World Heritage Site
    • Known for: Alpine flora and rare species like snow leopard and Himalayan musk deer
  • Valley of Flowers National Park (Chamoli)
    • Also a UNESCO World Heritage Site
    • Famous for: Over 500 species of wildflowers, scenic Himalayan beauty

Wildlife Sanctuaries / Forest Reserves:

  • Rajaji National Park (Haridwar, Dehradun, Pauri Garhwal)
    • Now a Tiger Reserve
    • Known for: Elephants, leopards, deer, and rich birdlife
  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary (Almora)
    • Known for: Himalayan oak forests, over 200 species of birds
    • Great for birdwatching and trekking
