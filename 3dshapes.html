Cube

A cube has 6 equal square faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices. All interior corners meet at exact right angles. (Euler's formula verified: V - E + F = 2).

Cuboid

A cuboid has 6 rectangular faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices. Also known as a rectangular prism, it resembles a standard box structure.

Sphere

A sphere is a perfectly symmetrical round structure. It has no edges and no vertices. Every single coordinate on the outer boundary is equidistant from its center.

Cylinder

A cylinder has 2 parallel circular bases and 1 curved outer wrapper. It has 2 circular edges and no sharp vertices.

Cone

A cone has 1 flat circular base, 1 curved wrap surface, and 1 vertex apex. It tapers smoothly from the circular base to the apex point.
