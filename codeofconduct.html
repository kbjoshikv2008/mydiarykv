Code of Conduct

Official guidelines, rules of conduct, and disciplinary protocols for KVS Teachers and Students

Article 59. For Teachers

The provisions of the CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964 shall apply mutatis mutandis to all the employees of the Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan. In addition to this, the following code of conduct shall also be applicable to teachers. Violation of these shall make an employee liable for action under the CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965.

  1. Every teacher shall, by precept and example, instill in the minds of the pupil, entrusted to his care, love for the motherland.
  2. Every teacher shall, by precept and example, inculcate in the minds of his pupils respect for law and order.
  3. Every teacher shall organize and promote all school activities which foster a feeling of universal brotherhood amongst the pupils.
  4. Every teacher shall, by precept and example, promote tolerance for all religions amongst his pupils.
  5. No teacher shall be a member of any political party or carry on activities either openly or in camera in support of any such party.
  6. The teacher shall always be on the alert to see that his pupils do not take active part in politics.
  7. No teacher shall be a member of the State or Central Legislature. He shall resign his job before contesting for elections as a candidate.
  8. Every teacher shall take a stand against unhealthy and bad customs and practices in modern society and must strive his best to instill in the minds of his pupils the principles of co‐operation and social service.
  9. Every teacher shall co-operate with and secure the co-operation of other persons in all activities which aim at the improvement of the moral, mental and physical well-being of pupils.
  10. Every teacher shall be strictly impartial in his relations with all his pupils. He shall be sympathetic and helpful particularly to the slow learners.
  11. Every teacher shall be a learner throughout his life not only to enrich his own knowledge, but also of those who are placed in his care.
  12. Every teacher shall regard each individual pupil as capable of unique development and help him to be creative as well as co-operative.
  13. Every teacher shall be temperate and sober in his habits, scrupulously avoiding undesirable habits within the precincts of the Vidyalaya.
  14. Every teacher shall have an exemplary moral character. His dealing with others shall always bring credit to the Vidyalaya.
  15. Every teacher shall take pride in his calling and try to promote the dignity and solidarity of his profession.
  16. Every teacher shall be an advocate of freedom of thought, expression and development of scientific temper.
  17. No teacher shall indulge in or encourage any form of malpractice connected with examinations.
  18. Confidential matters relating to the institution and the Department shall not be divulged by any teacher.
  19. No teacher shall undertake private tuition or private employment or otherwise engage himself in any business.
  20. Every teacher shall dress neatly, cleanly and in a dignified manner while on duty.
  21. Every teacher shall be punctual in attendance in respect of class work and Vidyalaya activities.
  22. Every teacher shall abide by KVS rules and regulations and show due respect to the constituted authorities.
  23. Every teacher shall avoid monetary transactions with pupils and parents, refraining from exploiting influence for personal ends.
  24. No teacher shall prepare or publish textbooks/keys or canvass for publishing firms/traders without prior Sangathan approval.
  25. It shall be the duty of every teacher to:
    1. Respect the National Flag and the National Anthem.
    2. Promote harmony, common brotherhood, and respect the dignity of women.
    3. Develop scientific temper, humanism, and the spirit of inquiry.
    4. Safeguard public property and abjure violence.
    5. Strive towards excellence in individual and collective activity.

Additional KVS Guidelines:

Every teacher shall ensure that he/she should not use mobile phones in classrooms to avoid disrupting the teaching process.

It is the responsibility of teachers to ensure the absolute security and safety of children under their control, escort, or supervision during excursions, tours, camps, and meets.

A teacher must maintain high ethical values and set a moral example, keeping professional boundaries and strictly avoiding any form of unwelcome or derogatory behavior.

Article 60. For Students

Acts of Misconduct:

  1. Misbehavior towards teachers, staff, or school employees.
  2. Intentional disturbance of classrooms or academic activities.
  3. Absence from classes without prior permission from teachers/Vice Principal/Principal.
  4. Bullying, intimidation, or harassment of other pupils.
  5. Eve-teasing or misbehavior towards fellow students.
  6. Damaging, defacing, or disfiguring school property.
  7. Propagating strikes, class disruptions, or communal divisions.
  8. Indulging in physical violence or disobeying lawful orders of authorities.
  9. Bringing unauthorized items or individuals inside the school premises.

Disciplinary Actions:

Based on the gravity of the misconduct, the Disciplinary Committee may recommend actions including:

  • Oral / written warnings to students and parents.
  • Suspension from attending classes or school for a specified duration.
  • Financial recovery for loss caused to school property.
  • Compulsory issue of Transfer Certificate (TC) or expulsion from the Sangathan.
