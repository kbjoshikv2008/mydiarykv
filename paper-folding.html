Paper Folding Geometry

Hands-on origami exercises to discover, visualize, and prove mathematical constructions

Back to Home
Perpendicular Bisector

Discover the midpoint and line of symmetry of a line segment by folding a sheet of paper.

  • Draw a line segment AB on tracing paper.
  • Fold the paper so that point A falls exactly on point B.
  • Crease the fold. The crease is the perpendicular bisector.
Angle Bisector

Construct an exact angle bisector crease without using a protractor or mathematical compass.

  • Draw an angle ABC on your paper.
  • Fold the paper so that ray BA lies along ray BC.
  • Press and crease along the fold line to define the bisector.
Parabola Envelope

Create a parabola envelope curve through folding tangents with respect to a focus point.

  • Draw a circle on paper and mark a focus point F inside.
  • Fold a point on the circle circumference onto point F.
  • Repeat for many points on the circle to form the envelope.
